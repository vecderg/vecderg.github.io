Sample Text

oEma: bay i dani e giso teso e sote.
oDani: pin e kwesote giso.
oEma: may a bay i gabi teso a bay i soy boti e san giso.
oDani: gwi!
oEma: bemyu, a bay i boti e kwedoy lina.
oDani: bay i dyu tewi e pin.

Vocabulary:

  • teso = who, that, which (relative clause marker)
  • gabi = to think, thought
  • gwi = good
  • lina = tongue, taste, lick
  • tewi = allow, let, permit


Relative Clauses

"teso" creates a relative clause like the English words "who", "that", and "which". It can either take an object to expand on its details by turning it into a subject (e.g. "giso teso e sote" = "soup that is hot") or take a verb to expand on the details of its complement (e.g. "bay i gabi teso a bay i soy boti e san" = "I think that I don't want that").
