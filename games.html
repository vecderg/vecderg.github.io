GAMING


My primary occupation is a game designer! I have a large collection of games that you can explore on my itch page, which has downloadable games and games that are playable in browser: https://vecderg.itch.io/

Despite primarily being a game designer, I touch on all aspects of game development in my number of solo-developed projects. I'll talk about some of my favorite projects, some of which were focused more on characters and others more on mechanics.

DRACOFIGHTER



If you want THE Vecderg game of all time... it's DracoFighter!

This game took me about 3 years to complete in between classwork and other projects, and as my dream project, it kind of carries a lot of my soul, if I can be dramatic. DracoFighter is a traditional fighting game meant to bare the genre down to its fundamentals, but without turning combat into something boring or gimmicky. It focuses on what makes fighting games so fun to me -- the decision-making and outplays -- by completely doing away with complex mechanics and inputs. This makes it a fighting game that's really appealing to beginners!

It also features hand-drawn animation of dragons, something I really wanted to be in the game. The story mode is meant to be silly and a bit carefree, while setting things up for the planned sequel...

The game is available on itch, Steam, and the Google Play Store, for Windows, Mac, Linux, and Android.

DEMON'S HEIR



Demon's Heir is a retro-styled 3D action platformer! You play as Beewee the ninja cat, who has incredible speed and combat prowess.

This is a single-level prototype I put together for class over the course of multiple months, but the game idea is something I've had for a long, long time. I actually finished a 2D version of the game, but it's a far cry from what I imagined the final product to be, so I'm not really encouraging people to play it... I want to be able to make a 3D platformer with smooth, quick-paced movement that also integrates a simple, dramatic storyline. We'll see if it ever gets finished! But for now, you can play the demo in browser or by download.

UnderwOtter



Despite visually being one of my most unpolished games, UnderwOtter is actually one of my favorite creations! It was made for Ludum Dare 48, where I can happily take my achievement of being 105th place for humor. With the theme being "Deeper", I fulfilled my childhood dream of having a diving game where you get treasure and exchange the money to keep going deeper...

Otto the Otter is a separate game I'll be working on, but I needed to take the chance to reuse my otter protagonist for this swimming game! I also reused my fishing dragon character who happened to be the perfect shopkeeper. The game loop requires you to use the most of your air meter to get treasure, and sell that treasure to be able to swim longer and faster. The fisherdragon will also ramble to you if you talk to him after upgrades, which I loved rereading after forgetting the lines. I'm actually still shocked that I finished this game in just 48 hours between the map, upgrade routes, and dialogue...
